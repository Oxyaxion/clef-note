# Atomic Habits

## The Four Laws

1. **Make it obvious** — design your environment to surface the cue
2. **Make it attractive** — bundle habits with things you enjoy
3. **Make it easy** — reduce friction, start with 2-minute versions
4. **Make it satisfying** — immediate reward anchors the loop

## What stuck

The identity-based framing is the most useful idea: instead of "I want to run a marathon", adopt "I am a runner". Behaviour follows identity.

**Habit stacking**: attach a new habit to an existing one.

> "After I pour my morning coffee, I will read for 10 minutes."

**Environment design** beats willpower every time. Put the guitar in the middle of the room. Put the phone in the other room.

## Criticism

The book is repetitive — the core ideas could fit in a long essay. Still worth reading for the examples and the systematic framing.
